The #1 Rule: Sell Transformation, Not Features

This is the single most important messaging principle. Never lead with features (hours of training, page counts, number of templates). Lead with the outcome and transformation the prospect will experience. People don't want an 8-hour course. They want the result.

If your copy describes WORK the prospect has to do, rewrite it. Sell what happens AFTER the work.

Features go on the back end after they're in. The front end is 100% about transformation, outcomes, and benefits.

Messaging Examples

GOOD - TRANSFORMATION FOCUSED
"How 150+ agency owners built a consistent pipeline so they never have to wonder where the next client is coming from"
GOOD - OUTCOME + AUDIENCE
"For agency owners doing $30K+/month: the exact playbook to double your MRR in 12 months"
GOOD - AGITATES A REAL PAIN POINT
"Your revenue is up but your take-home is down. Here's the system to fix that."
GOOD - EMOTIONAL OUTCOME
"Stop working nights and weekends to keep your agency alive. Get the roadmap to consistent revenue so you can finally feel in control."
BAD - SELLING WORK, NOT OUTCOMES
"Get 8 hours of video training and a 120-page implementation workbook"
BAD - FEATURE DUMP, NO TRANSFORMATION
"Access proven frameworks, templates, and strategies to transform your business"
BAD - SELLING PLANNING (PEOPLE DON'T PAY TO PLAN)
"Join our $97 workshop to plan your next year and map out your goals"
BAD - GURU ENERGY, TOO HYPED
"UNLOCK YOUR POTENTIAL AND 10X YOUR BUSINESS NOW!!!"

Ad Creative Rules

The Contrast Framework (Required for All Copy)

Every ad and landing page must walk the prospect through this sequence. This is what converts lukewarm and cold traffic, not just the red-hot buyers who already know the brand.

# Step What It Does
1 Where are they now? Meet them in their current pain (overworked, inconsistent pipeline, take-home shrinking)
2 What have they tried? Acknowledge what hasn't worked (hiring, courses, doing it alone)
3 Why hasn't it worked? Explain the gap (no system, no accountability, wrong approach)
4 How is it not their fault? Remove shame so they stay engaged (nobody taught you this, you're not broken)
5 What's the outcome they can get? Paint the transformation: consistent pipeline, financial control, freedom, confidence

Without contrast, you're just listing features. Features convert brand loyalists. Contrast converts everyone else.

Red-hot buyers will convert no matter what. Your copy needs to win the lukewarm and cold traffic too.

Ad-to-Page Congruence (Non-Negotiable)

If you rewrite the landing page, you must rewrite the ads. If you rewrite the ads, the landing page must match. People should never click an ad and feel confused when they land on the page. Mismatched messaging kills conversions.

The Scroll-Stop Test

Every ad must answer three questions in under 3 seconds:

1. Who is this for? (Agency owners doing $30K+ MRR)
2. What's the transformation? (Specific outcome, not features or vague "frameworks")
3. Why should I care right now? (Urgency, scarcity, or pain point)

DO
  • Lead with transformation and outcomes, not features
  • Name the audience ("agency owners doing $30K+/mo")
  • Use the Contrast Framework (pain, gap, outcome)
  • Include testimonials, story, and social proof
  • One clear CTA per creative
  • Match ads to landing page messaging exactly
  • Use professional photography of real events
  • Match the website aesthetic (dark, clean, premium)
DON'T
  • Lead with features (hours of video, page counts, template counts)
  • Sell "work" (8 hours of training, planning sessions)
  • Use vague language ("frameworks", "strategies" without context)
  • Use conflicting CTAs ("Register" AND "Purchase" in same ad)
  • Run new ads to old landing pages (or vice versa)
  • Use stock photos or generic business imagery
  • Make it look like a course or info product launch
  • Leave room for interpretation (be crystal clear)

Workshop and Webinar Ad Standards

Paid workshops ($97) require extra clarity because the prospect is making a purchase decision, not just registering for a free event. The copy must sell the transformation, not the agenda.

The Workshop Problem: Don't Sell Planning

If your workshop ad reads like "pay $97 to plan your year," you've already lost.

Business owners already plan their year. They don't see themselves paying to do something they already do for free. You have to sell the OUTCOME of the workshop, not the activity.

SELL THIS
  • "Start the year with a running start while everyone else plays catch-up"
  • "Get the exact 90-day blueprint our members use to add $10K+ MRR"
  • "Walk away with a clear path to consistent pipeline"
  • The transformation and confidence they'll feel after
NOT THIS
  • "Plan your next year" (that's selling work)
  • "8 hours of training videos" (that's selling more work)
  • "120-page implementation workbook" (that's homework)
  • Any copy that describes effort the prospect has to put in

Required Elements

Element Requirement
Audience Call-Out Must explicitly state who this is for (e.g., "For agency owners doing $30K+/month")
Transformation / Outcome What will they become or achieve? Not "frameworks" but what life looks like after. Consistent pipeline, financial control, clarity.
Contrast Where are they now vs. where they could be. Without contrast, there's no reason to act.
Social Proof Testimonials, member results, or story. Without social proof, lukewarm traffic won't convert.
Single CTA One action only. "Get Your Seat" or "Register Now." Never both.
Brand Colors Red (#CF1F36), White, Black only. Match website aesthetic.

Workshop Ad Formula

[AUDIENCE] + [PAIN/CONTRAST] + [TRANSFORMATION] + [PROOF] + [CTA]

Example: "Agency owners doing $30K+/month: you're working harder than ever but your take-home keeps shrinking. 150+ agency owners fixed this with a proven system. Get the blueprint. $97 - Limited seats."

Funnel and Landing Page Rules

Rule Why
Most valuable real estate (top of page) must SELL, not name the product If someone reads the header and leaves, you wasted your best shot. Sell the outcome, not the product name.
Minimize friction between click and conversion Double opt-ins, extra steps, and extra forms create drop-off. Click button, go to order form. Keep it simple.
Once they're on the checkout page, STOP selling Same as a sales call: once you've presented the offer, shut up. Tell them what they get, show the form, let them buy. Don't re-sell beneath the order form.
Ads and landing pages must be congruent If you rewrite the page, rewrite the ads. Mismatched messaging confuses people and kills conversions.

Common Mistakes

Mistake Why It Fails Fix
Selling "plan your year" or "plan your growth" People already plan for free. Why would they pay $97 for something they do themselves? Sell the outcome: "Start the year ahead of your competition with a proven growth blueprint"
Leading with features (8 hours of video, 120-page workbook) You're selling them WORK. 8 hours of video sounds like a commitment, not a benefit. Lead with transformation. Save feature details for after they're already interested.
No testimonials, no story, no contrast Red-hot buyers convert anyway. But lukewarm and cold traffic needs proof and emotional connection. Add member success stories, before/after results, and the contrast framework.
Re-selling below the checkout form Creates doubt. If you're still trying to convince them on the checkout page, it undermines confidence. Checkout page = what you get + order form. That's it. Stop selling.
Running old ads to a new landing page Message mismatch. People click expecting one thing and land on another. Conversions tank. Every page rewrite needs matching ad rewrites. Congruence is non-negotiable.

Free Offer and Lead Magnet Standards

Free offers still need to SELL. The fact that it's free doesn't mean the copy can be lazy. Better copy sifts and sorts for higher-quality leads, which means better calls and higher close rates downstream.

The Feature Trap

When you're too close to the product, you default to describing features. This is natural, but it kills conversion. The prospect doesn't want 8 hours of videos. They want what those 8 hours will give them: a consistent pipeline, financial control, the ability to provide for their family without burning out.

TRANSFORMATION-FOCUSED
  • "Get a proven roadmap to consistent client acquisition"
  • "Stop guessing and start growing with a clear plan"
  • "The system 150+ agency owners use to scale without burning out"
  • Focus on how they'll FEEL: in control, confident, clear
FEATURE-FOCUSED
  • "8 hours of video training"
  • "120-page implementation workbook"
  • "Access to templates and frameworks"
  • Anything that sounds like homework or effort

Reverse-engineer the copy from the outcome backward.

What does the prospect actually want? Consistent pipeline. Providing for their family. Feeling in control. Feeling safe. Start there, not with "8 hours of video."

Pre-Launch Checklist

Run every ad creative and landing page through this checklist before going live:

# Check
1 Does it clearly identify the audience? (Agency owners, $30K+ MRR)
2 Does it sell TRANSFORMATION, not features? (No leading with hours, pages, or templates)
3 Does it use the Contrast Framework? (Where they are now vs. where they could be)
4 Does the top of the page SELL, not just name the product?
5 Is there social proof? (Testimonials, member stories, results)
6 Is there only ONE CTA?
7 Are the ads congruent with the landing page? (Same message, same promise)
8 Is the checkout page clean? (No re-selling below the order form)
9 Are we using brand colors only? (Red, White, Black)
10 Does the image match the message?
11 Is the path to purchase as frictionless as possible? (Minimal steps)
12 Would a $30K+/month agency owner stop scrolling for this?
