Typography

Our typography is clean, bold, and modern. Headers are large and commanding. Body text is readable and professional.

Element Style
Headlines Bold (700-800 weight), large, uppercase or title case. Sans-serif only.
Body Text Regular weight (400-500), 16-20px, clean sans-serif
CTAs Bold, clear, action-oriented. One CTA per creative.
Fonts Allowed System sans-serif, Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Helvetica, Arial
Fonts NOT Allowed Script fonts, decorative fonts, Comic Sans, anything "playful"

Ad Creative Rules

The Scroll-Stop Test

Every ad must answer three questions in under 3 seconds:

1. Who is this for? (Agency owners doing $30K+ MRR)
2. What do I get? (Specific outcome, not vague "frameworks")
3. Why should I care right now? (Urgency, scarcity, or pain point)

DO
  • Lead with a specific pain point or outcome
  • Name the audience ("agency owners doing $30K+/mo")
  • Use real numbers and results ($247M, 150+ members)
  • One clear CTA per creative
  • Use professional photography of real events
  • Show social proof (member results, testimonials)
  • Match the website aesthetic (dark, clean, premium)
DON'T
  • Use vague language ("frameworks", "strategies", "systems" without context)
  • Use conflicting CTAs ("Register" AND "Purchase" in same ad)
  • Use stock photos or generic business imagery
  • Use bright/flashy colors outside our palette
  • Make it look like a course or info product launch
  • Leave room for interpretation (be crystal clear)
  • Use images that don't connect to the offer

Image and Photography Guidelines

Workshop and Webinar Ad Standards

Paid workshops ($97) require extra clarity because the prospect is making a purchase decision, not just registering for a free event.

Required Elements

Element Requirement
Audience Call-Out Must explicitly state who this is for (e.g., "For agency owners doing $30K+/month")
Desired Outcome What will they walk away with? Be specific. Not "frameworks" but what those frameworks accomplish.
Price Justification If there's a fee ($97), make clear what they're paying for and the value they receive
Single CTA One action only. "Register Now" OR "Get Your Seat" - not both "Register" and "Purchase"
Social Proof Member count, revenue results, or testimonial snippet
Brand Colors Red (#CF1F36), White, Black only. Match website aesthetic.

Workshop Ad Formula

[AUDIENCE] + [PAIN POINT] + [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] + [PROOF] + [CTA]

Example: "Agency owners doing $30K+/month: your revenue is up but your take-home is shrinking. Join 150+ agency owners who fixed this with our pricing and packaging system. $97 - Limited seats."

Common Mistakes in Workshop Ads

Mistake Why It Fails Fix
"Access proven frameworks" Vague. What frameworks? What do they do? "Get the exact client acquisition system our members use to add 5+ clients/month"
"Templates you use Monday" Unclear. Use for what? What happens Monday? "Walk away with a plug-and-play outbound system you can deploy this week"
Image of "Onboarding Workshop" unrelated to offer Visual doesn't match the message Use event photos, Josh presenting, or results-focused visuals
"Register Now" + "$97" as separate CTAs Conflicting actions confuse the prospect Single CTA: "Get Your Seat - $97"

Pre-Launch Checklist

Run every ad creative through this checklist before going live:

# Check
1 Does it clearly identify the audience? (Agency owners, $30K+ MRR)
2 Does it agitate a specific pain point?
3 Is there a clear, specific outcome promised?
4 Is there only ONE CTA?
5 Does the image match the message?
6 Are we using brand colors only? (Red, White, Black)
7 Is the copy crystal clear with no room for interpretation?
8 Does it include social proof? (member count, results, testimonial)
9 Would a $30K+/month agency owner stop scrolling for this?
10 Does it feel premium, not "guru course"?
