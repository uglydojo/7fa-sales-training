Quick reference for ad creatives

01

Brand Colors

Three colors. That's it. Red, White, and Black. Every creative should feel clean, bold, and premium.

No pastels. No neon. No gradients. If it's not in this palette, don't use it.

02

Visual Standards

DO
  • Use real photos from SFA events and intensives
  • AI-generated content is fine if it matches our brand feel and tone
  • Highlight key text or elements for emphasis
  • Dark backgrounds with white text
  • Get creative, you have autonomy within these guardrails
DON'T
  • Deviate from our brand colors and fonts
  • Use colors, fonts, or styles that don't feel like us
  • Think slapping our logo on a creative makes it on-brand
  • Use script fonts, decorative fonts, or anything "playful"
  • Make it look like a course launch or guru pitch

You have creative freedom. The guardrail is simple: it has to feel like us.

Match our website's look and tone: dark, clean, premium. If you stripped the logo off, would someone still know it's SFA? That's the test.

03

Messaging Tone

We sound like a trusted advisor, not a guru. Confident, not hyped. Our audience is sophisticated, they've seen every pitch. We need to earn their attention.

We are a coaching and mentorship community (done-WITH-you). We are NOT a done-for-you service.

We deliver proven playbooks, strategies, and systems. Our members do the work. Never imply we do it for them.

Lead With Avoid
Specific outcomes and results Vague promises ("transform your business")
Real pain points (take-home shrinking, wearing too many hats) Feature dumps (8 hours of video, 120 pages)
Social proof (150+ members, $247M in results) Hype language (UNLOCK, 10X, CRUSH IT, GAME-CHANGER)
Clear audience call-out ("agency owners doing $30K+/mo") Generic messaging that could apply to anyone
04

Pre-Launch Checklist

Run every creative through this before it goes live:

1
Uses only our brand colors (Red, White, Black, Dark Gray)?
2
Visuals match our brand feel (AI content is fine if it looks like us)?
3
Clearly identifies the audience (agency owners, $30K+ MRR)?
4
Leads with an outcome or pain point, not features?
5
Has only ONE clear CTA?
6
Feels premium, not cheesy or guru-ish?
